# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**Hosts** — friends, family members, wedding party organizers who create and manage event pages. They need a clear dashboard to create events, track uploads, share guest links, and download photos. They may not be technical. They're organizing a real-life event and need the tool to stay out of their way.

**Guests** — event attendees who upload photos from their phones. No account, no install. They're in the moment at a wedding or celebration. The upload flow should feel like contributing to a shared memory, not filling out a form.

## Product Purpose

Memoire lets anyone collect wedding and event guest photos without friction. Hosts create a temporary event page, share a QR code, and guests upload images from their phones without signing up. The upload path is direct-to-storage so the server never touches photo binaries. Success is a host downloading a full album of candid guest photos they'd never have seen otherwise.

## Brand Personality

Romantic, moody, intimate. Three words: **warm, generous, intentional**.

Voice is human, not corporate. No jargon, no hype. Speaks like someone hosting a dinner party, not running a SaaS.

## Anti-references

- Overdone wedding sites: pastels, script fonts, lace textures, doily ornamentation. Too sweet.
- Generic SaaS dashboards: blue/white cards, heavy borders, corporate-minimal. Too cold.
- Social media patterns: Instagram grids, story circles, like/comment buttons. Not a social app.

## Design Principles

1. **Two modes, one product.** The host dashboard should feel capable and clear. The guest upload page should feel warm and emotional. Design each for its context, not for consistency at all costs.

2. **Moody warmth.** Dark but not cold. The palette should feel like a candlelit room, not a terminal. Romantic, film-stock richness.

3. **Frictionless generosity.** The guest should never think about the tool. Uploading is an act of giving, not a task. Every interaction removes a reason to hesitate.

4. **Intimate scale.** This is not a social platform. It's a private collection for specific people and specific moments. Design for the small, meaningful group.

5. **Anticipate, don't ask.** The product should know what comes next. Prefill, preselect, progress automatically when safe.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Adequate contrast ratios, keyboard navigable, focus indicators. No critical interaction relies on color alone. The guest upload experience should work well for older attendees (larger touch targets, clear labels, forgiving input).
